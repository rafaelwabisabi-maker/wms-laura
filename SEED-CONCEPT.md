## The Big Idea

**You already built it. That's the hard part. Now name it, price it, and sell it.**

WMS-Laura is a warehouse management system (WMS) built for Laura's logistics business. It runs. It works. It has real data flowing through it. The gap between "custom project for one client" and "productized SaaS" is: a landing page, a pricing page, and 3 referrals.

**The market:** Small and medium logistics businesses (5-50 employees) in Austria and Germany are caught between:
- Enterprise WMS systems (SAP, Oracle) — too expensive, too complex
- Spreadsheets + phone calls — too chaotic, prone to errors

There is almost nothing in between. WMS-Laura fills that gap at a price point that works: €300-500/month per client.

## Revenue Model

**Monthly subscriptions:**
- Basic (€299/month): 1 warehouse, 5 users, 500 orders/month
- Pro (€499/month): 3 warehouses, 20 users, unlimited orders, API access
- Enterprise (€899/month): Multi-location, custom integrations, priority support

**Annual contracts:** 20% discount for annual payment upfront = cash flow positive from Day 1.

**Revenue target:**
- Month 3: 5 clients = €2,495/month
- Month 6: 15 clients = €7,485/month
- Month 12: 30 clients = €14,970/month (~€180K ARR)

**The boring truth:** 30 clients at €500/month is a viable, fundable, acquirable business. It's not sexy. It's not viral. It doesn't need to be. Logistics SaaS businesses sell for 4-6x ARR. €180K ARR = €720K-1.08M exit.
